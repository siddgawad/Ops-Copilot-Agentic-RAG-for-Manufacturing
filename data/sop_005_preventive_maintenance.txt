SOP-005: Preventive Maintenance Schedule — CNC Machines
Department: Maintenance
Equipment: All CNC Machining Centers and Lathes
Revision: 5.0
Last Updated: 2025-08-30

1. PURPOSE
This procedure defines the preventive maintenance (PM) schedule for all CNC machines in the facility. Adherence to this schedule reduces unplanned downtime by up to 60%, extends machine life, and maintains part quality within specification.

2. DAILY MAINTENANCE (Performed by Operator at Start of Shift)

Task 2.1: Check Way Lube Level
- Inspect the way lube reservoir (typically located on the left side of the machine).
- The level should be between the MIN and MAX lines on the sight glass.
- If below MIN, add Mobil Vactra Oil No. 2 (ISO VG 68).
- Do NOT use any other oil type. Incorrect way lube causes stick-slip on the linear guides.

Task 2.2: Check Hydraulic Pressure
- Verify the hydraulic pressure gauge reads between 50-70 bar.
- Pressure below 45 bar indicates a pump issue or a leak. Report to Maintenance immediately.
- For machines with hydraulic chucks or tailstocks, low pressure means the workpiece is not properly clamped. DO NOT RUN THE MACHINE.

Task 2.3: Check Coolant Level and Concentration
- Refer to SOP-002 (Coolant System Maintenance) for detailed instructions.
- Minimum actions: Verify coolant level is above the sump LOW marker. Check concentration with refractometer.

Task 2.4: Clean Chip Trays and Conveyors
- Remove chip trays and empty accumulated metal chips into the chip recycling bin.
- Clear any chip buildup around the tool changer arm, spindle nose, and axis covers.
- Chip buildup on axis covers can damage the accordion-style way covers (replacement cost: $2,000-5,000).

Task 2.5: Check Air Pressure
- The facility air supply should read between 6.0-7.0 bar at the machine regulator.
- Air pressure below 5.5 bar can cause the tool changer to malfunction (ATC fault).
- If air pressure is low, check for leaks at the quick-connect fittings. Listen for hissing sounds.

3. WEEKLY MAINTENANCE (Performed by Maintenance Technician)

Task 3.1: Lubricate Linear Guides and Ballscrews
- Apply grease (Mobilgrease XHP 222) to all grease points on the X, Y, and Z axes.
- Each axis has 2 grease points (one per linear guide rail). Total: 6 points per machine.
- Use a grease gun with a standard Zerk fitting. Apply 3-5 pumps per point.

Task 3.2: Check Spindle Belt Tension
- Open the spindle access panel (top of the machine).
- Press down on the belt midway between the pulleys with approximately 10N of force.
- Deflection should be 5-8mm. If deflection exceeds 10mm, tighten the belt using the tensioner adjustment bolt.
- If the belt shows cracks, fraying, or glazing, replace it. Estimated belt life: 5,000 spindle hours.

Task 3.3: Test Axis Backlash
- Using a dial indicator mounted on the spindle, measure backlash on each axis:
  - Jog the axis 10mm in the positive direction. Zero the indicator.
  - Jog the axis 10mm in the negative direction, then back 10mm positive.
  - The indicator reading is the backlash.
- Acceptable backlash: Less than 0.015mm for machining centers, less than 0.008mm for precision grinding.
- If backlash exceeds limits, adjust the ballscrew preload or replace the ballscrew nut.

Task 3.4: Inspect Toolchanger (ATC)
- Run the tool changer through a full carousel rotation (manually cycle through all tool pockets).
- Listen for unusual sounds (grinding, clicking, hesitation).
- Verify that the tool change time is within specification (typically 1.5-2.5 seconds chip-to-chip).
- Lubricate the ATC arm pivot points with light machine oil.

4. MONTHLY MAINTENANCE

Task 4.1: Check Spindle Runout
- Install a precision test bar in the spindle.
- Mount a dial indicator on the table and measure runout at 50mm and 200mm from the spindle face.
- Acceptable runout: 0.005mm at 50mm, 0.010mm at 200mm.
- Increasing runout over time indicates bearing wear. Track trends in the PM Log.

Task 4.2: Clean and Inspect Electrical Cabinet
- Open the electrical cabinet (LOTO required).
- Use compressed air (max 30 PSI) to remove dust from circuit boards, drives, and fans.
- Check all cable connections for looseness.
- Verify cooling fan operation. A failed cabinet fan can cause servo drive overheating and random faults.

Task 4.3: Calibrate Axes (Laser Interferometer)
- Every 6 months, use a Renishaw XL-80 laser interferometer to measure and compensate axis positioning accuracy.
- Acceptable linear accuracy: ±0.005mm over the full axis travel.
- Update the linear error compensation table in the machine control after calibration.

5. DOCUMENTATION
- All PM tasks are recorded in the CMMS (Computerized Maintenance Management System — currently using Fiix).
- Work orders are auto-generated by CMMS based on this schedule.
- Completion of PM tasks must be signed off by the performing technician and verified by the Maintenance Supervisor.
- PM compliance rate target: 95% or higher per month. Current facility average: 91%.
